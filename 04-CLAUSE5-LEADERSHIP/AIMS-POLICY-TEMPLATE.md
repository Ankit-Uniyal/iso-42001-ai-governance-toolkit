## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This AI Management System (AIMS) Policy establishes [Organization Name]'s commitment to the responsible development, deployment, operation, and governance of Artificial Intelligence (AI) systems in alignment with ISO/IEC 42001:2023 — Artificial Intelligence Management Systems.

This policy provides the overarching framework under which all AI-related activities are governed, ensuring that AI systems are developed and used in a manner that is safe, transparent, fair, accountable, and aligned with applicable laws, regulations, and ethical principles.

### 1.2 Scope

This policy applies to:
- All AI systems developed, procured, deployed, or operated by [Organization Name]
- All employees, contractors, consultants, and third-party suppliers involved in AI-related activities
- All business units, divisions, and subsidiaries within the AIMS scope
- Third-party AI systems integrated into organizational processes or products

**AIMS Scope Statement:** [Define specific organizational boundary — e.g., "The design, development, deployment, and monitoring of AI-powered products and services across [Business Unit/Division], including third-party AI APIs and models integrated into customer-facing applications."]

## 2. Policy Statement

[Organization Name] is committed to:

1. **Responsible AI Development** — Designing and developing AI systems that are safe, reliable, robust, and aligned with human values and organizational ethics
2. **Transparency and Explainability** — Ensuring that AI system decisions and behaviors are understandable, explainable, and communicated appropriately to affected parties
3. **Fairness and Non-Discrimination** — Identifying, assessing, and mitigating bias in AI systems to prevent discriminatory outcomes affecting any individual or group
4. **Human Oversight and Control** — Maintaining meaningful human oversight of AI systems, particularly in high-stakes decision-making contexts
5. **Privacy and Data Protection** — Processing personal data in AI systems in compliance with applicable privacy laws and protecting the rights of data subjects
6. **Security and Resilience** — Protecting AI systems from adversarial attacks, unauthorized access, and misuse
7. **Legal and Regulatory Compliance** — Ensuring all AI systems comply with applicable laws, regulations, and standards including the EU AI Act, GDPR, and sector-specific requirements
8. **Continual Improvement** — Continuously improving the effectiveness and maturity of the AIMS through regular review, audit, and improvement cycles

## 3. AI Governance Principles

### 3.1 Ethical AI Principles

All AI systems developed and operated by [Organization Name] shall adhere to the following ethical principles:

**Human-Centric Design** — AI systems shall be designed with human wellbeing, dignity, and autonomy as primary considerations. Technology serves human needs, not the reverse.

**Beneficence** — AI systems shall be designed to provide benefit to users, customers, and society. Potential harms shall be identified, assessed, and mitigated before deployment.

**Justice and Fairness** — AI systems shall treat all individuals and groups equitably. Discriminatory outcomes based on protected characteristics (race, gender, age, disability, religion, national origin, sexual orientation, etc.) are prohibited.

**Autonomy and Agency** — Users affected by AI decisions shall retain meaningful agency. Where AI is used in consequential decisions, humans shall retain the ability to understand, challenge, and override decisions.

**Accountability** — Clear accountability for AI system outcomes shall be established and maintained. AI systems shall not be deployed in ways that diffuse responsibility or prevent accountability.

**Transparency** — The use of AI shall be disclosed appropriately. Users interacting with AI systems shall be informed of this fact. Decisions affecting individuals shall be explainable.

### 3.2 Prohibited AI Uses

[Organization Name] prohibits the following uses of AI:
- Social scoring systems that evaluate individuals based on behavior and assign scores affecting rights or opportunities
- Real-time remote biometric identification in publicly accessible spaces (except as permitted by law)
- Manipulation of individuals through subliminal techniques to influence behavior against their will
- Exploitation of vulnerabilities of specific groups (age, disability, social/economic situation)
- AI systems designed to deceive users into believing they are interacting with humans
- Any application of AI classified as prohibited under the EU AI Act (Article 5) or other applicable law

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities

### 4.1 Top Management

Top management shall:
- Demonstrate visible leadership and commitment to the AIMS
- Ensure the AIMS is aligned with organizational strategic direction
- Ensure adequate resources are allocated for AIMS implementation
- Promote a culture of responsible AI development and use
- Review AIMS performance at planned intervals
- Approve this AI Policy and ensure it is communicated throughout the organization

### 4.2 Chief AI Officer (CAIO) / AI Governance Lead

The CAIO / AI Governance Lead shall:
- Manage day-to-day AIMS operations and oversight
- Chair the AI Governance Committee
- Report AIMS performance to top management
- Ensure implementation of AI governance controls across all AI projects
- Maintain awareness of AI regulatory developments and update AIMS accordingly

### 4.3 AI Governance Committee

The AI Governance Committee shall:
- Review and approve AI systems for deployment
- Oversee AI risk assessments and treatment plans
- Review and approve AI system impact assessments (ASIA)
- Escalate significant AI risks and incidents to top management
- Meet at least quarterly

### 4.4 AI System Owners

AI System Owners shall:
- Maintain accountability for their assigned AI system's governance
- Ensure AI risk assessments are conducted and documented
- Implement approved risk treatment controls
- Report AI incidents and performance issues promptly
- Ensure human oversight mechanisms are operational

### 4.5 Data Protection Officer (DPO)

The DPO shall:
- Review AI systems that process personal data
- Ensure Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) are conducted where required
- Advise on privacy-by-design in AI development
- Coordinate with supervisory authorities on AI-related privacy matters

### 4.6 All Employees

All employees involved in AI activities shall:
- Comply with this policy and all AIMS-related procedures
- Complete required AI governance training
- Report potential AI risks, incidents, or policy violations promptly
- Use AI tools only in accordance with approved acceptable use policies

## 5. AI Risk Management

### 5.1 Risk Assessment Requirement

[Organization Name] shall conduct risk assessments for all AI systems within the AIMS scope prior to deployment and at defined intervals thereafter. Risk assessments shall consider:
- Bias and fairness risks
- Transparency and explainability risks
- Data quality and privacy risks
- Security and adversarial attack risks
- Operational risks (performance drift, failures)
- Regulatory and compliance risks
- Societal and reputational risks

### 5.2 AI System Impact Assessment (ASIA)

An AI System Impact Assessment shall be conducted for all AI systems before deployment. The ASIA shall assess:
- Potential impacts on individuals and affected communities
- Societal impacts including effects on vulnerable groups
- Impacts on fundamental rights and human dignity
- Environmental impacts
- Opportunities and benefits

### 5.3 Risk Appetite

[Organization Name] operates with the following AI risk appetite:
- **Critical risks (score 15-25):** Zero tolerance — immediate treatment required
- **High risks (score 10-12):** Low tolerance — treatment plan required within 30 days
- **Medium risks (score 5-9):** Moderate tolerance — treatment plan required within 90 days
- **Low risks (score 1-4):** Acceptable within defined monitoring parameters

## 6. AI Lifecycle Requirements

### 6.1 Design and Development

All AI systems shall be designed with:
- Responsible AI principles embedded from inception (ethics by design)
- Privacy-by-design and security-by-design principles
- Bias evaluation and mitigation at data, algorithm, and output levels
- Explainability mechanisms appropriate to the risk level
- Human oversight mechanisms for high-stakes applications
- Documented design specifications and architectural decisions

### 6.2 Testing and Validation

Prior to deployment, all AI systems shall undergo:
- Functional testing against defined performance requirements
- Bias and fairness evaluation across relevant demographic groups
- Adversarial robustness testing
- Testing in representative/production-equivalent environments
- Security testing and penetration testing where applicable

### 6.3 Deployment

AI systems shall only be deployed when:
- All required risk assessments and impact assessments are completed
- All critical and high risks are treated to acceptable residual levels
- AI Governance Committee approval is obtained
- Monitoring and alerting mechanisms are operational
- Incident response procedures are in place
- User disclosure and transparency requirements are met

### 6.4 Operation and Monitoring

Deployed AI systems shall be:
- Monitored continuously for performance, accuracy, and fairness
- Reviewed for model drift and data distribution changes
- Audited against governance requirements at defined intervals
- Subject to incident response procedures for anomalies or failures

### 6.5 Decommissioning

When AI systems are retired:
- Training data shall be securely deleted or anonymized per data retention policy
- Model weights and artifacts shall be archived or securely deleted
- Dependent systems and integrations shall be updated
- Affected stakeholders shall be notified

## 7. Data Governance for AI

### 7.1 Data Quality

AI training data shall meet defined quality standards including completeness, accuracy, representativeness, and timeliness. Data quality assessments shall be documented.

### 7.2 Data Provenance

The provenance and lineage of AI training data shall be documented, including data sources, collection methods, licensing terms, and any transformations applied.

### 7.3 Bias in Data

Training data shall be evaluated for bias. Where bias is identified, mitigation measures shall be implemented and documented. Bias evaluation results shall inform risk treatment decisions.

### 7.4 Personal Data

Personal data used in AI systems shall be processed only with a valid legal basis under applicable privacy law. Data minimization, purpose limitation, and subject rights shall be respected. AI models shall not retain or reproduce personal training data.

### 7.5 Data Access Controls

Access to AI training datasets and model artifacts shall be controlled on a least-privilege basis. Access logs shall be maintained.

---

## 8. Transparency and Disclosure

### 8.1 User Disclosure

Users shall be informed when they are interacting with an AI system, except where the AI nature of the interaction is obvious. Disclosure shall be clear, conspicuous, and provided before interaction commences.

### 8.2 AI Capabilities Information

Accurate information about AI system capabilities, limitations, and performance characteristics shall be provided to users and deployers. Material limitations shall not be concealed.

### 8.3 Explainability

For AI systems that make or influence consequential decisions affecting individuals, appropriate explainability mechanisms shall be implemented. Users shall have access to meaningful explanations of AI-influenced decisions affecting them.

### 8.4 Incident Communication

When AI incidents occur that affect users, customers, or other stakeholders, communication shall be made in a timely, transparent, and accurate manner in accordance with the AI Incident Response Procedure.

---

## 9. Third-Party and Supply Chain AI

### 9.1 Third-Party AI Assessment

All third-party AI systems, APIs, and models considered for adoption shall undergo a documented risk assessment before integration. Assessment shall cover technical performance, bias and fairness, security, privacy compliance, and regulatory status.

### 9.2 Contractual Requirements

Contracts with AI suppliers and providers shall include:
- AI governance and responsible AI requirements
- Right to audit and access documentation
- Incident notification obligations
- Data protection requirements
- Specification of the supplier's role (provider vs. deployer under applicable law)

### 9.3 Ongoing Monitoring

Third-party AI systems in use shall be monitored for performance changes, security vulnerabilities, and compliance risks on an ongoing basis.

---

## 10. Human Oversight

[Organization Name] shall ensure that AI systems used in high-stakes decisions include effective human oversight mechanisms. These mechanisms shall allow competent humans to:
- Understand AI system outputs and the factors influencing them
- Override AI decisions where appropriate
- Disable or suspend AI systems presenting unacceptable risk
- Review and challenge AI-influenced decisions

The level of human oversight required shall be proportionate to the risk level of the AI application, with higher-risk applications requiring more direct and active oversight.

---

## 11. Training and Awareness

All personnel involved in AI activities shall receive training appropriate to their role, including:
- AI ethics and responsible AI principles
- AI risk assessment methodology
- Applicable regulatory requirements (EU AI Act, GDPR, sector-specific)
- AI incident identification and reporting
- AI-specific job role requirements

Training requirements shall be assessed annually and updated to reflect changes in technology, regulation, and organizational context.

---

## 12. Compliance and Enforcement

### 12.1 Regulatory Compliance

[Organization Name] shall maintain awareness of and comply with all applicable AI-related laws and regulations, including but not limited to:
- EU Artificial Intelligence Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689)
- General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) / UK GDPR
- Sector-specific AI regulations (healthcare, financial services, etc.)
- ISO/IEC 42001:2023 AI Management Systems

### 12.2 Policy Violations

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or contract. Serious violations may be reported to regulatory authorities as required by law.

### 12.3 Exceptions

Exceptions to this policy require written approval from the CAIO and documentation of the justification and compensating controls. Exceptions shall be reviewed at least annually.
