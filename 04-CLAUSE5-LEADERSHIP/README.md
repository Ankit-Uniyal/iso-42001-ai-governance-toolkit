# Clause 5 — Leadership
## ISO/IEC 42001:2023 | Implementation Guide

> **Purpose:** Top management must actively lead and commit to the AIMS — not just sign off on it.

---

## 5.1 — Leadership and Commitment

### What it requires
Top management must demonstrate **active, ongoing commitment** to the AIMS.

### Evidence of Commitment Auditors Accept

| Action | Evidence |
|--------|---------|
| Establish the AI policy | Signed policy, distribution records |
| Align AIMS with strategy | Board papers referencing AI governance |
| Ensure resources | Budget approvals, headcount, tools |
| Communicate importance | All-hands decks, CEO emails, intranet posts |
| Promote improvement | Management review actions, improvement logs |

### Implementation Steps
1. Appoint a board-level sponsor (Chief AI Officer, CTO, or equivalent)
2. Include AI governance as a standing agenda item in leadership meetings
3. Allocate a defined budget for AIMS implementation and maintenance
4. Establish an AI Ethics/Governance Committee with executive representation
5. Have the CEO communicate the AIMS launch to all staff

### Documents Required
- Leadership Commitment Statement (signed by CEO)
- AIMS Budget Allocation Record
- AI Governance Committee Terms of Reference
- Management communication evidence (emails, presentations, meeting minutes)

## 5.2 — AI Management Policy (AIMS Policy)

### What it requires
Top management must establish, implement, and maintain an AI policy that:
- Is appropriate to the organisation's purpose
- Provides a framework for setting AI objectives
- Includes commitment to satisfy applicable requirements
- Includes commitment to continual improvement of the AIMS

### What the AIMS Policy Must Include

1. Statement of purpose — why you use AI and what you aim to achieve
2. Ethical principles — fairness, transparency, accountability, human oversight
3. Commitment to compliance — ISO 42001, GDPR, EU AI Act, and other applicable laws
4. Commitment to continual improvement — not a static document
5. Scope reference — consistent with AIMS Scope Statement from Clause 4.3
6. Commitment to protecting individuals from harmful AI outcomes

### AIMS Policy Template

    [ORGANISATION NAME] — AI MANAGEMENT POLICY
    Version: 1.0 | Approved by: [CEO] | Date: [Date] | Review: Annual

    1. PURPOSE
    [Organisation] uses AI to [business purpose]. This policy establishes our
    commitment to managing AI responsibly, ethically, and in compliance with
    applicable requirements.

    2. SCOPE
    This policy applies to all AI systems within our AIMS scope:
    [list key systems or functions from Clause 4.3].

    3. ETHICAL PRINCIPLES
    We commit to:
    - Fairness: AI shall not discriminate unlawfully
    - Transparency: We will be open about AI use in decisions affecting people
    - Accountability: Every AI system has a named owner
    - Human Oversight: Humans remain in control of high-risk AI decisions
    - Privacy: Personal data handled per applicable law
    - Safety: AI systems will not cause unacceptable harm

    4. COMMITMENTS
    - Comply with ISO 42001 and all applicable AI laws and regulations
    - Maintain and continually improve our AIMS
    - Allocate adequate resources for responsible AI management
    - Engage with affected stakeholders transparently

    5. RESPONSIBILITIES
    Top Management: Overall accountability
    AI Governance Lead: Day-to-day AIMS management
    All Staff: Awareness and compliance

    Signed: [CEO Name, Title] | Date: [Date]

### Implementation Steps
1. Draft policy using the template above
2. Consult with legal, HR, and AI teams
3. Approve at board level
4. Distribute to all staff and publish on intranet
5. Include in new employee onboarding
6. Review annually or after major regulatory changes

- **AI-MANAGEMENT-POLICY.md** — Full AIMS Policy (see file in this folder)

## 5.3 — Organisational Roles, Responsibilities and Authorities

### What it requires
Top management must assign and communicate responsibilities and authorities for:
- Ensuring AIMS conforms to ISO 42001
- Reporting AIMS performance to top management
- Specific AI system ownership and accountability

### Key Roles to Define

| Role | Responsibilities | Reports To |
|------|-----------------|-----------|
| AI Governance Lead / CAIO | Overall AIMS, policy owner, regulatory interface | CEO / Board |
| AI System Owner | Accountable for specific AI system performance and compliance | CAIO |
| AI Developer / MLOps | Technical implementation, model docs, bias testing | System Owner |
| Data Privacy Officer | AI + personal data compliance (GDPR interface) | Legal / CAIO |
| Internal Auditor | AIMS internal audits per Clause 9.2 | Audit Committee |
| Risk Manager | AI risk register, treatment plans | CAIO / CRO |
| HR / People Lead | AI competence, training, awareness programmes | CAIO / CHRO |

### RACI Matrix for AIMS Activities

| Activity | AI Gov Lead | System Owner | Developer | DPO | Risk Mgr |
|---------|-------------|--------------|-----------|-----|---------|
| Policy maintenance | R/A | C | I | C | I |
| AI risk assessment | A | R | C | C | R |
| Incident response | A | R | R | C | C |
| Internal audit | A | I | I | I | I |
| Supplier assessment | A | R | C | C | I |
| Management review | R | C | I | C | C |

R=Responsible, A=Accountable, C=Consulted, I=Informed

### Implementation Steps
1. Create an org chart showing all AI governance roles
2. Write role descriptions for each AI governance position
3. Publish and communicate the RACI matrix to all relevant staff
4. Ensure every AI system has a named, accountable owner
5. Include AI governance responsibilities in formal job descriptions

### Documents Required
- AI Governance Roles and Responsibilities Document
- RACI Matrix for AIMS Activities
- AI System Ownership Register (person accountable for each AI system)

## What Auditors Check in Clause 5
- Can top management explain the AIMS policy without being prompted?
- Is there a named person with real authority responsible for AIMS?
- Is AI governance funded — is there a visible budget?
- Is the policy current, signed, and accessible to all staff?
- Does every in-scope AI system have a named, accountable owner?
- Are roles documented and do people actually know their responsibilities?
