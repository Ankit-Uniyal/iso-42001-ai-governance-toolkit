## Purpose

This log records proactive improvement opportunities identified for the AIMS — improvements that go beyond fixing nonconformities (which are tracked in NCR-REGISTER.md). It demonstrates that the organisation is continuously improving the suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of the AIMS, as required by Clause 10.2.

---

## Difference Between Corrective Action (10.1) and Continual Improvement (10.2)

| Aspect | Corrective Action (10.1) | Continual Improvement (10.2) |
|--------|--------------------------|------------------------------|
| Trigger | Something went wrong | Proactive identification of better ways |
| Nature | Reactive — fixing a failure | Proactive — making things better |
| Requirement | Required when nonconformity occurs | Required even when things are working |
| Example | Fixing a missing impact assessment process | Adopting better bias testing tools before required |

## Improvement Sources

Improvements may be identified from:
- Management Review outputs (Clause 9.3)
- Internal audit observations (Clause 9.2) — observations, not just NCRs
- AI performance trends (Clause 9.1) — areas not yet failing but deteriorating
- Stakeholder feedback and requests
- New AI governance best practices or standards updates
- Industry incidents and lessons learned from peers
- Staff suggestions and innovation
- Emerging regulations (e.g., new EU AI Act requirements)
- Technology improvements (better explainability tools, fairness metrics)
- Benchmarking against ISO 42001 maturity models

## PDCA Cycle for Improvements

For each improvement, follow the Plan-Do-Check-Act cycle:

- **PLAN:** Identify the opportunity, set the objective, plan the change
- **DO:** Implement the improvement (pilot or full rollout)
- **CHECK:** Monitor and measure the results against the expected benefit
- **ACT:** If effective, standardise and embed across the AIMS. If not effective, revise and try again.
